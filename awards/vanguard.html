The Vanguard Awards has 4 sub-categories that recognise the creativity, innovation, effort and talent of WKWSCI students through their works.

Introduced in 2021, the Young Entrepreneur honours students' works that embody the tenacious entrepreneurial spirit of WKWSCI and whose innovative work is aimed at making a positive contribution to the school and/or local community.*Awarded by panel of judges

The Esthétique recognises students' works with significant aesthetic appeal and a lasting impact on their audience. *Awarded by panel of judges

Previously known as the Risk-taker & Groundbreaker categories, the Trailblazer celebrates students' works that are pioneers in challenging the status-quo and dare to put themselves in precarious positions to cover controversial issues. *Awarded by panel of judges

Lastly, the People’s Choice is the top voted submission among all other submissions for the Vanguard sub-categories.*Awarded by vote

Perspectives Film Festival 2020

Cheong Jun Rui (CS'22), Cheryl Chua Xin Ting (CS'21), Nikki Andrea Bounaparte (CS'23), Clara Toh En Ci (CS'21)
Esthétique Award Nominee
Previous Next
Click here to view work

Perspectives Film Festival 2020 welcomed new conventions of film-viewing in its thirteenth instalment, pushing the boundaries of a physical festival into digital territory and reconfiguring physical ancillary programmes to the comforts of viewers’ homes. In conveying the festival theme of “Truth”, design team members developed a distinctively sophisticated brand identity through a monochromatic colour palette. This was reflected across all publicity material and digital assets for the virtual festival, which included an interactive website, social media collateral, merchandise and print assets. These conceptualisations were part of WKWSCI’s Film Festival Practicum, where undergraduates from NTU curate and organise an annual thematic film festival in Singapore.

The team wanted to challenge universal notions of factuality through colour symbolism and visual associations. Conveyed through the use of blacks and whites and serif typefaces, and opposed with muted colours and sans serif typefaces, the branding intentionally confuses the arbitrary dichotomy of truth as fact and fiction in an introspective tone. Topped with a swirled visual, it seeks to challenge the convention of truth as a linear consequence, and symbolises truth as a malleable medium.

Genesis

Bryan Tan Yong Ern (CS'22), Fontanilla Base Eryka Joyce (CS'22), Ou Jie Hui Joy (CS'22), Tan YanYing Nikki (CS'22)
Esthétique Award Recipient
Previous Next
Click here to view work

In examining the layers and nuances of self, we question our identity and get lost in the whirlwind of personas we put forward. It seems as though a different version of ourselves exists for different circumstances and thus, we perform to conform. But with each performance we bring to the surface a piece of ourselves, and they all come together to form a beautiful whole.

With the film touching on the personal subject of individuality, the film mainly capitalizes on close-ups of the dancer to create a sense of intimacy between the audience and the subject. This includes shots of her neck, arms, and legs as she moves. The film also aims to use the vibrancy (or lack thereof) of the different locations to capture the subject’s mental state. For instance, the slowness and darkness of the underwater shots will be used to convey uncertainty and the feeling of being stifled, whereas the brightness and openness of the garden reflects a sense of rebirth and renewal. While none of us have truly attained the self-assuredness we seek, this film shows you what we think it feels like.

Genesis is a performative documentary on the journey of self-discovery, interpreted through dance.

Trailblazer Award Nominee

As part of the Regional Strategic Communication Management module, under the guidance of Prof Sonny Rosenthal, the team visited Indonesia and partnered with a non-profit organisation, The Learning Farm. The Learning Farm helps vulnerable youths who are at risk of being trapped in a cycle of poverty, crime, drugs and HIV/AIDS. These youth are given opportunity to learn agriculture and other life skills at their facility.

Seeds of Change is a three-part docu-series produced, directed and filmed by the team. The series showcases the positive impact of The Learning Farm on its students and alumni. Their videos have since been used as part of their publicity and outreach efforts. The total views have amounted close to 1,700 views on their YouTube channel.

The team also provided video making crash courses during our visit, equipping the non-profit organisation with the skillset to continue making their own impact videos. They have since produced their own updated impact video, featuring more alumni and students.

The Glass Prison

Grace Lee Jia Wei, Nur Khairiyah Binte Atan, Tan Wen Shan, Tracey Ang Xinhui, Junita Ryu Ji Yeon (All CS'24)
Trailblazer Award Nominee
Previous Next
Click here to view work

The Glass Prison tells the story of Saul, an ex-convict who has been given multiple chances at life. Behind his cheerful facade hides a man who once struggled to break out of the glass prison: societal stigma against ex-convicts like himself.

In theory, once ex-offenders serve their time, it is a rightful completion of a just punishment. But in reality, most find themselves thwarted with unfair biases and judgement by the general public. The discrimination they face is subtle but pervasive. Through this story, the team wanted readers to empathise and understand that at the core — ex-convicts are humans too. The fact that they have served time in prison does not make them any less of a human. Whilst they cannot undo their misdeeds, we can learn to embrace them with compassion.

Dive deep into the story of Saul, where the work takes you on a visual and emotional journey through his hopes and feelings in both the past and present.

Seeing Through the Fog

Shintaro Tay Chin Hoe (CS'22)
Trailblazer Award Nominee
Previous Next
Click here to view work

While being masked-up, Shintaro struggles to see through the viewfinder because the fog from his breath would gather on his spectacles. His heavy breaths are a result of nervousness that comes from the fear of missing the moment. Throughout the past year, he was driven by how journalism is seen as a first draft of history and he wanted to contribute his own voice to this collective narrative. Without much accreditation or access, he strived to document the extraordinary in the everyday responses to the pandemic. Photojournalism has been a way for him to make sense of what is happening to the people around. At the same time, it allows him to reflect upon how our lives have changed and have them as a record of our times. This body of work is a collection of curiosity in Shintaro's quest to understand how humanity continues amidst the coronavirus pandemic.

Ron Yap Chen Xiong (CS'22)
mentalhealthceo

Young Entrepreneur Award Nominee


Click here to view work

Ron Yap Chen Xiong (CS'22) runs @mentalhealthceo, a mental health awareness page on Instagram with more than 12,000 followers. He is also trained in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), and he utilizes the page to run his own mental health coaching business, helping others overcome their anxiety, depression and struggles in general using CBT.

His strategy is as follows: He first uses Instagram growth techniques such as hashtag research using flick.tech and viral content to attract users to his page. Apart from having them follow him, he also uses his bio link to attract users to a free lead magnet, which is a short resource on anxiety. The link then leads on to his further offers, including a full 130-page ebook called How To Overcome Anxiety and an offer to hop on a 1 hour counselling call with him. This strategy is called a marketing funnel.

Through the free lead magnet, he would also obtain the user’s email, which he would then put into his database for email marketing using more advanced email marketing techniques using ActiveCampaign to remarket his ebook and his coaching.

He would then use the profits to reinvest into Instagram advertising, creating a successful funnel loop.

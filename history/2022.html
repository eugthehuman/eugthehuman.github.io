WKWSCI Spotlight Awards 2022

The annual WKWSCI Spotlight Awards recognises the outstanding work, creativity and spirit of excellence from our students and alumni in the dynamic field of communication and information. In tandem with WKWSCI’s 30th anniversary, the awards will spotlight and celebrate the many achievements of our students - past and present. Following the previous edition, Spotlight Awards 2022 boasts a wide range of award categories including the Vanguard Awards and the Special Recognition Awards. New to this year’s awards, the APRW Excellence Award will be given out to the top FYP works in collaboration with APRW, one of Singapore’s leading integrated marketing communications agencies.

'Soaring Solos' is a community-building campaign that focused on improving the quality of lives of Single Unwed Mothers in Singapore. 'Soaring Solos' strived to connect Single Unwed Mothers with one another, as well as with organisations that would best understand their situation and be of help to them. At the same time, the project sought to improve the flow of information and resources to these mothers, so that these mothers can make well-informed decisions quickly.

The Role of Selective Exposure in Preserving Decision Diversity and Group Performance

Peh Jia Wang
Top Final-Year Project (Research)


Click here to view FYP repository

Previous research has shown that group decisions obtained by aggregating independent and diverse decisions are typically more accurate than individual decisions. Although decisions in the real world are not independent due to social influence, group decisions have performed well. This project examines why group decisions perform well in the real world despite social influence by considering the countervailing effects of selective exposure, and highlights the usefulness of selective exposure amidst negative portrayals in extant literature.

The Grubs

Pearlyn Chang Xuan Qi
Watanabe Shiya
Top Final-Year Project (Broadcast)


Click here to view FYP repository

The Grubs is an animated fantasy short film about Jane, a teenage girl who gets sucked into a vending machine and is forced to navigate through the magical workings of an entire society of Grub people that exist to power vending machines on the island.

The film explores dark themes such as exploitation, loneliness & isolation, and social divide as the Grub people exist as a metaphor for the migrant worker community in Singapore. Under the guide of a bright and bold art style, similar to that of a children’s novella, “The Grubs” takes you on a an emotional roller coaster from start to end.

Tan Tam Mei

CS'15
Thailand Correspondent at The Straits Times

Thailand Correspondent Tam Mei has been reporting for The Straits Times from Bangkok since June 2021. Before being posted overseas, she spent time at the paper's features desk, and covered manpower, security, crime and social-related stories as well.

One of her highlights include a scoop on the Singapore government allowing the use of medical cannabis for select child patients with epilepsy. This multi-story package involved trips to South Korea and Thailand to report on their medical weed regulations. She was also involved in the paper's coverage of the Myanmar coup, and other notable events including the general elections and the fatal SCDF ragging incident in 2018.

She cut her teeth as a cub reporter at The New Paper where she covered the crime, court and government policy beats. Her current coverage in Thailand ranges from the latest political events, like the power moves within the coalition and opposition parties, and social issues such as the children's rights' crisis arising from the pandemic.

She also writes on a wide range of human interest stories, with on-the-ground interviews of people living in Thailand. For example, the impact of the persistent street protests on low-income residents living near these sites, and the severe and social fallout due to the lack of vaccines and medical care when the pandemic first started.

Drop a Deuce with Duo

Nicole Lim, Chong Weng Tai Abner, Tan Jia Huey Madeline (All CS'22)
Esthétique Award Recipient
Previous Next
Click here to view work

People find it hard to do their daily lessons on Duolingo due to a lack of “free time”. However, 90% of people bring their phones along with them to the toilet, playing games, reading, and scrolling through social media. Turns out, everyone has free time… when they drop the deuce. The campaign matches the daily habit of dropping the deuce with daily language lessons. With lenticular posters, out-of-home and AR filters, the team will remind users that learning a language is easy, just Drop a Deuce with Duo.

Drop a Deuce with Duo was also submitted to the D&AD 2022 New Blood Awards, one of the largest competitions for emerging creatives to exercise and showcase their talents on briefs set in partnership with some of the world’s leading brands.

Shark Sense

Ong Chong Li Philip (CS’24), Lim See Mun (CS’22), Tang Yi Ting (CS’22)
Trailblazer Award Recipient
Previous Next
Click here to view work

In a world of fitter, faster, stronger, better; they asked themselves -- what about 'together'? As part of the CS4070 Issue in Advertising module, the team got to pitch an idea to the global fitness brand, Gymshark. They wanted to come up with a product or advertising idea that would help communities break significant barriers they faced to conditioning.

Identifying the blind community as one that faces significant challenges keeping fit and going to the gym, the team felt that the blind community had every right, just like anyone else, to visit the gym and maintain a healthy lifestyle. Combining creativity and research, they came up with 'Shark Sense', a necklace line that can detect and describe the gym for a visually impaired user. It integrates visual technology from Microsoft's Seeing AI -- an application designed for the low vision community.

The project idea was recognised by AKQA and has been shortlisted for the upcoming Future Lions Global Advertising Award Competition.

WeeVolunteer, together with Calm Collective Asia

Shermaine Yeo Jie Rui (CS’23), Eryka Fontanilla (CS’22), Glen Foong (CS’22), Sherrie See (CS’24), Lim Hui Min, Clara (CS’23), Liam Wei Shan Erin (CS’24), Sih Jesslyn (CS’23), Tang Chen-Xi, Joy (CS’23), Goh Yit Chen, Kellyn (CS’23), Teo Sze Rae Heather (CS’24), Hannah Soh (CS’24), Chong Rui Xuan (CS’25), Ellisha Chua Hui Xuan (CS’25), Soh Ji Yun (CS’25)
Young Entrepreneur Award Recipient
Previous Next
Click here to view work

Under WeeVolunteer, this team partnered with Calm Collective Asia, a mission-driven organisation that aims to break the stigma of mental health in Asia to create a campaign that supports the organistion’s existing social media presence.

Amidst the pandemic, mental health and wellbeing have been a growing concern in Singapore and around the world. Inspired to contribute in their ways, this campaign was a meaningful collaboration that allowed the team to put their communications skills to practice for an important cause that has a direct impact on those around us.

The team took charge of the full campaign from ideation, design, production and copywriting, working together to bring the content to life. The collaterals encompassed Calm Collective's message of normalising mental health conversations in society, with each piece of content specially constructed to educate the public, as well as encourage anyone going through difficult times to push through. They also explored the use of creative and new methods to engage with their audience, consisting of deliverablessuch as short-form videos, an Instagram filter and Telegram stickers, apart from the usual Instagram posts. The team also contributed content to the organisation’s newly launched TikTok account.
